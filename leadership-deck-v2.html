Slide 03 — Our Solution
What We Actually Build for the Client
A simple, thin layer that connects the client's existing online store to AI shopping assistants. We don't change their store. We just make it visible to AI.
Simple Analogy
Think of food delivery apps. When a restaurant joins Uber Eats, DoorDash, and Zomato — they don't build 3 kitchens. They add a tablet that receives orders from all platforms and sends them to the same kitchen.

Our accelerator is that tablet.
The client's SAP Commerce is the kitchen.
Google, OpenAI, Microsoft are the delivery apps.
🍴
One Kitchen. One Tablet. All Platforms.
We don't change the kitchen (SAP).
We add the tablet (accelerator).
All delivery apps (AI platforms) send orders through it.
How It Works
STEP 1
Customer Talks to AI
"Buy me a Dyson V15"
via Gemini, ChatGPT, or Copilot
→
STEP 2
AI Calls Our Layer
Wipro's accelerator receives the request, translates it
→
STEP 3
SAP Processes It
Client's existing store handles pricing, stock, cart, order
→
STEP 4
Customer Gets It
Payment via Google Pay/Stripe. Order confirmed. Shipped.
What We Touch vs What We Don't
Wipro Builds (the thin layer)
  • A small set of API endpoints (the "tablet")
  • Translates AI platform language → SAP language
  • Translates SAP responses → AI platform language
  • Tracks shopping sessions
  • Handles customer account linking (OAuth2)
  • ~500 lines of code. 5-6 endpoints. That's it.
We Do NOT Touch
  • Client's SAP Commerce configuration
  • Product catalog or pricing rules
  • Discounts, promotions, or tax rules
  • Inventory or warehouse systems
  • Payment processing (Google Pay / Stripe handle this)
  • Zero business logic changes. Zero risk to existing store.

Slide 07 — Appendix
Anticipated Leadership Questions
Answers to the questions that will come up.
Market & Strategy
QuestionAnswer
Is this a real trend or hype?Google, OpenAI, and Microsoft all launched commerce protocols within weeks of each other. Walmart, Visa, Mastercard, Shopify are backing it. $385B projected by 2028. This is real.
Will one protocol win?Too early to call. Our approach is protocol-agnostic — we support all of them. Like building a website that works on all browsers.
What if this doesn't take off?Low investment risk. First client = 2 developers for 10 weeks. If it works, we have a practice. If it doesn't, we've built API and cloud skills that are useful elsewhere.
Why would clients pay us and not do it themselves?Same reason they pay us for SAP implementations. Protocol knowledge, mapping expertise, and pre-built templates save them months. We deliver in 10 weeks; internal teams take 6+ months.
Can't SAP or Shopify build this natively?They will — in 12-18 months. Clients who wait lose a year of AI commerce sales to competitors. We bridge the gap.
Technical (Simplified)
QuestionAnswer
Do we need to integrate with each AI platform separately?No. We expose endpoints on the client's domain. AI platforms call us. We just register on each portal (like listing a restaurant on Uber Eats). The actual code is 90% shared.
Is this secure? What about payments?Payment never touches our layer. Google Pay and Stripe handle it with tokenized cards. Our layer only passes product/cart data. No credit cards, no PII stored.
What if the client's SAP goes down?Our layer returns an error to the AI agent, which tells the customer. Same as what happens when a website goes down. We don't add a new point of failure — we depend on what already exists.
Do we change anything in the client's SAP?Zero changes. We only call their existing SAP REST APIs (which are already exposed for their website/app). No configuration, no business logic, no data changes.
Why not use MuleSoft or another integration platform?If the client already has MuleSoft, we use it. But buying a $100K/year license for 5 API endpoints is like buying a truck to deliver a letter. A simple custom API is right-sized.
